Income Streams

As we all know from the 10X Rule, and general common sense, passive income and constant cash flow/replenishment makes more sense than relying on the uncertainty of small month-to-month paychecks. Diversification is key, as anyone with half a brain realises. I have listed below all of the methods I personally use/have previously used to build ‘wealth’. Note that I am certainly no millionaire however, standard millionaire/billionaire practice can be easily applied to smaller, more socially standard household wealth. I’ve also included brief notes on my personal cash management.

Passive Income

  • Stock, Index, Commodity & Cryptocurrency Portfolio: I maintain a diversified folio which has seen as-high-as quarterly return of 65%
  • Bonds & Savings Accounts: I own two fixed rate £5,000 bonds and use a savings account to hold larger sums of money for consistent ‘quaranteed’ income
  • Real Estate: Something I look forward to getting into when I have the money. Most relaible and consistent income but requires spare cash I lack

Labor Income

  • Self-Employment: I have nothing against good ol’ fun labor. I’ve had a good share of enjoyable jobs
  • Other Employment: With my little confectionery business, I made more money monthly than I have in any ‘wagey’ job
  • Freelancing Talents: If I see any opportunities to sell my talents, I don’t know document transcribing or something, I don’t waste the opportunity

Cash Locations

  • Clydestale (Main) Current Account: Where all my payments income (jobs, passive income etc.) and payments outgo (dependencies, bills etc.)
  • Starling (‘Spendy’) Current Account: Where I send myself money to spend on a regular basis. Able to view as separate, more managable, figure
  • M&S Credit Account: I make most large purchases with this account to gain rewards and get my shopping for free!
  • Clydestale Savings Account: This came with my Clydestale Current Account with the same bank and pays a fixed rate. Again, just a good place to keep cash
  • Barclays Fixed Rate Bonds: My two bonds aren’t very risky and therefore have AER of 0.25%. Just a ‘safe’ place to store cash
  • eToro Trading Account: eToro is where I keep my money for trading in stocks, indices, commodities and cryptocurrencies
  • Self-Invested Personal Pension (SIPP): I have invested my first sum in a SIPP. It’s based on an externally managed medium-risk equity and bond folio
