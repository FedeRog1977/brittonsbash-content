These recipes are my daily drivers and I take great pride in perfecting these to my taste. I have typical weekday recipes for function (i.e. the all-5,000-grams-of-carbs diet to fuel the hills) and, more expansive weekend recipes for enjoyment. The former is based on minimising ingredients and costs. I like to rely on consistently cheap and functional ingredients, not the inconsistency of ‘special offers’. I aim to consume relatively similar foods during the week for effective economies of scale. I tend to spend approximately £20 per week on weekday ingredients. The latter is based on more complex ingredients, methods and flavours; using a greater number of ingredients and generally more time. My budget for these tends to also be approximately £20.

Okay, here's some shopping beta for you. From Monday to Friday I tend to buy the same ingredients: oats and accompanyments for breakfast, snacks, and lunch, and an array of vegetables, beans and meats for dinner (with which I vary recipes). These ingredients are inexpensive. As I say, I spend approximately £20 a week on weekday food, all-inclusive (pantry stuff etc.). So in my eyes, I can be as ‘extravagant’ as I want with these. For example, if I want to buy M&S Oakham chicken breasts instead of Sainsbury’s Basics ‘chicken breasts’, there may be a slight difference in price but the product is of the same sort, will be higher quality (which I’m willing to pay for), and the overall price range of my product options will remain the same. So, I don't consider my budget based on finite prices of products, I base my budget on the range of prices in which my chosen products are available; maintaining a consistent average price and price variance. This means I have the option to eat high quality food, which is always based on raw and whole-form ingredients, for a fair price. And, I can adjust this budget accordingly. I have no doubt in saying many people who do their weekly shop in Iceland or some crap probably spend more than I do on my weekly M&S shop. Stop spending a retraded amount on your shopping; just do what I do.
