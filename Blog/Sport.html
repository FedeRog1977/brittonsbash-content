In July 2020, I bought a road bike. This road bike is my life, my love, my everything. He’s called RoGeR. He’s a handsome and mature man who is capable of anything his user puts him up to. The name comes, obviously, from the GoAT Roger Federer however, the letter case makes reference to LaTeX which implies skill, art, technique and precision. Much like Roger himself. See the website header for a link to my Strava and keep up with my roadie’ing. Although my main sport is road cycling, I do enjoy the frequent bag of a Munro or Corbett. I won’t say no to a Waterfoot lap either. These are also on Strava for your own viewing pleasure. My favourite sport is tennis but no one will satisfy my need for an opponent. So... if you want to be that person, hit me up on a Walkhighlands DM!

    Diet & Nutrition

    Daily Nutrition

    Note that this accounts for my maintenance only. I very rarely consume only this much as I tend to burn anywhere between, an additional, 200 to 2000 calories a day while cycling, hiking or walking.

    • Calories: 1,800
    • Carbohydrates (60%): 270g
    • Protein (20%): 90g
    • Fat (20%): 40g

    Taking into account these quantities, my typical 30 mile cycle may burn approximately 400 calories making my relative intake: 2,200Cal, 330C, 110P, 50F. If I’m sending a crazy marathon project in the hills, I may burn an additional 2000 calories making my relative intake: 3,800Cal, 570C, 190P, 85F. As you can see however, this often ends up being a lot more protein and fat than required so I’ll tend to substitute with even more carbs.
