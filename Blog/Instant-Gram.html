SUMC Cobble | 2nd of October

I didn’t think I’d be back on The Cobbler for a third time this year after The Playground and The Last Day of Summer however, it of course never disappoints. It was also good to finally see The Cobbler in the clag. I’ve only conquered this hill in clear, sunny conditions so it was good to experience the ESE face in slightly more awkward conditions. I say this was a SUMC walk but I did the ascent on my own coz I wanted the chad 120bpm run-in heart rate and I don’t believe in the ‘taking breaks’ hoax but, was joined by three excellent gents on the descent. The route took me almost three hours, hitting three segment PRs on the way. I have no clue how good that time is overall though coz I’ve never done this hill on it’s own. So yes, I did have Narnain, Ìme, Vane and Vorlich blue balls by the end of it.

Glencoe VI: The Mountain Goat | 24th-25th of September

A perfect trip from the start. It was sad not to see the Saab pull up for the drive-in but after just five minutes of the full FIVE SERIES experience, I was sold. An excellent drive-in and a very comfortable trunk camp after some sushware. A good sharp start on Saturday into the greatest walk-in of all time. Bidean did not dissapoint. Although the entire day was enclosed by the ooah clag clug, there was a bit of everything which made the entire route very engaging and rewarding. Good to be back on the Massif which broke me in May and, this time, embrace it’s magnificence. P.s. Let’s hope the Clachaig makes a full recovery.

Last Day of Summer? | 8th of September

The plan for this one was to add two new things into one of my favourtie routes. Surprisingly, I’d never actually been to the Narnain trig so the plan was to take the trad Narnain path from the A83 which crosses over the trig and all three cairns. Then, to drop down the Ben Arthur (The Cobbler) via the tourist staircase and follow the usual route on to Beinn Ìme. After this, I’d drop form Beinn Ìme to Beinn Luibhean, the corbett to the south west of it then do an A83 walk-out. All went to plan until I saw how disgusting the traverse looked; lose all your height, followed by fort of bogs, followed by fort of bogs vertical edition. So, I just came back down the tourist path and forestry commission access. Great day still, highs of 34° and a stunning coffee at Slanj.
